Manifesto Poster

Created as an assignment for Core 1 Typography at Parsons School of Design. The Fluxus Manifesto poster is an exploration of typography as a primary visual. The design utilizes a key phrase from the manifesto, "non-art reality", that is then expressed typographically to emulate the meaning of the movement and manifesto.

The Fluxus movement focused on the process of creating art rather than the actual art itself, using phrases such as "anti-art" and "living art" to explore definitions of art, mediums, and self-expression. To reflect this, I used overprinting to show distinct layers of type and visualize the idea of a "non-art reality".

Typeface: Courier
