A_Guide_To_Morse_Code

Visual Identity

2024

A pocket-sized book bound using a coptic stitch that acts as a guide to communicating in and understanding Morse Code. The book uses found text to outline the historical and modern uses of Morse Code to educate a younger generation on a dying language. The website acts as an extension of the book by creating a functional way for people to practice and communicate in Morse Code, offering uses the ability to translate English messages into Morse and transmit audio recordings.

The typographic system in the book and website uses underscores between words as a nod to the way the lenght of dash, in Morse Code, is used to space out letter during transmission. The graphic language is made up of a grid of dots that then utilizes negative space to convey the headers of each section in Morse.
